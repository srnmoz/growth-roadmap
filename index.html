Ad hoc & cross-team guardrail — 70 / 30

About 70% of capacity goes to planned roadmap work; up to 30% to high-priority ad hoc requests from Product, Engineering, Partnerships, Brand, Agency, events, launches and leadership.

Ad hoc work must support ARR, acquisition, visibility, conversion, authority or urgent risk reduction. Beyond the buffer, requests are queued, traded off, or escalated for reprioritisation.

What we stop. Net-new blog volume for its own sake · sitewide optimisation passes · unscoped support work · zombie experiments with no metric or kill date.

Big bets, major changes, risks and operating choices

what changes this year
LensDetailed direction
Big bets
  • +60% QoQ paid signups: 6,653 → 10,645. This is a bet, not a forecast.
  • Own research programme: citable reports from the ~1M-site install base, positioned as the thing competitors cannot copy and AI assistants can cite.
  • AI Search visibility definition: formalise GEO / AEO for cookieyes.com with a prompt set and tracker logic.
  • Agentic workflows: make O3 a full operating objective, with 4 validated experiments adopted and 1 live agent per role.
  • Wikipedia / entity presence: unified CookieYes page kicked off in Q2 and aimed for Q3.
  • Developer and agent-readiness content: MCP, skills and connectors as a visibility entry point.
Major changes from last year
  • Work is classified under five measured tiers: Acquisition, Visibility, Conversion, Authority and Efficiency.
  • Track A / Track B separates growth-initiated roadmap work from product-triggered support.
  • The 70 / 30 capacity guardrail is explicit, with queue-or-escalate handling when work exceeds the buffer.
  • Conversion is owned by the whole team, with each person accountable for paid-conversion impact from their own work.
  • Acquisition and Visibility have named metric owners: Shreya on Acquisition and Litto on Visibility.
  • The plan starts from a recovery footing after post-migration and March core update impact on top-3 positions.
Risks to manage
  • Research dependency: legal and data-team sign-off is the long pole.
  • Conversion capacity: Safna's conversion items flex with compliance-pod load; Shreya is the backup.
  • External dependencies: Rijo tool sign-off, signups data access, engineering for tech-SEO / LP builds, Alconost for translated video, and Growth Team 3 for agent-ready website work.
  • Wikipedia risk: entity work depends on independent coverage that still needs to be earned through press and research.
  • Measurement risk: Team 1 ARR target remains $XM, and attribution reliability after the June migration needs to be treated as a workstream risk.
  • Growth risk: +60% growth from a recovering channel needs clear framing as a stretch bet against Q1 actuals.
Stopping
  • Net-new blog volume for its own sake.
  • Broad sitewide optimisation passes with no cluster or conversion reason.
  • Unscoped support work that quietly consumes roadmap capacity.
  • Zombie experiments with no owner, metric, review date or kill date.
  • Programmatic SEO, parked to Q3+ until recovery is stable.
Doubling down
  • Cluster recovery toward pre-migration top-3 positions.
  • Proprietary research and entity authority, including the research asset and Wikipedia groundwork.
  • Agentic workflows and AI-assisted operations across every role.
  • Conversion-first video on money pages, with one shoot feeding multiple surfaces.
  • Developer / agent-readiness content as emerging-trend capture for MCP, skills and connectors.
