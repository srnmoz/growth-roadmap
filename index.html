Ad hoc & cross-team guardrail — 70 / 30

About 70% of capacity goes to planned roadmap work; up to 30% to high-priority ad hoc requests from Product, Engineering, Partnerships, Brand, Agency, events, launches and leadership.

Ad hoc work must support ARR, acquisition, visibility, conversion, authority or urgent risk reduction. Beyond the buffer, requests are queued, traded off, or escalated for reprioritisation.

What we stop. Net-new blog volume for its own sake · sitewide optimisation passes · unscoped support work · zombie experiments with no metric or kill date.

Three outcome-led objectives mapped to the roadmap's five tiers. Nine KRs, each classified as leading or lagging and marked committed or aspirational.

How to read this set

Lagging KRThe outcome we want. Slow, influenced by many factors, confirms success after the fact.
Leading KRThe input we control that predicts the lagging KR. Fast, steerable inside the quarter.
Committed100% must hit.
Aspirational80% attainment counts as success, and should still feel like a genuine stretch at the start.
Confidence1–10 at quarter start, updated weekly.
[brackets]Number still to confirm before lock.
Baseline convention Flow metrics baseline off Q1 achieved, Apr–Jun total, targeting the Jul–Sep total: ARR contribution, paid signups, organic clicks, commercial-page traffic, referring domains. State metrics baseline off a start-of-Q2 snapshot around 1 July using a trailing 28-day reading, targeting the 30 Sep snapshot: top-3 keyword count, CTR, Share of Authority.
